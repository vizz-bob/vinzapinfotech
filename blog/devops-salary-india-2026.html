DevOps Engineer Salary in India 2026 — Complete Guide

By Vijayendra Singh·February 2026·9 min read
← Back to Blog

DevOps engineering consistently ranks among the top five highest-paying IT roles in India. If you are considering a career in DevOps — or are already in the field and wondering how your salary compares — this guide gives you a realistic picture of what you can expect to earn in 2026, based on data from job portals, our student placement network, and industry salary surveys.

DevOps Salary by Experience Level

Experience is the single biggest driver of DevOps salaries in India. Here is what the data shows across seniority levels in 2026:

Good News for Freshers

Even without work experience, a fresher who has completed a structured DevOps training program with real project work can command ₹6–8 LPA at mid-size IT companies. Our students regularly achieve this within 60–90 days of completing their training.

Salary by Company Type

The type of company you work for can make a dramatic difference to your compensation package:

  • Product companies (startups and unicorns) — typically pay 20–40% more than service companies, plus offer ESOPs that can add significant value at IPO. Mid-level engineers at companies like Razorpay, Zepto, or Meesho can earn ₹25–40 LPA.
  • Large IT services companies (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCL) — salaries are more standardized and lower: ₹8–18 LPA for mid-level roles, but with job security, large scale exposure, and strong brand value on your resume.
  • Global MNCs (Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta) — top of the market. Mid-level DevOps / SRE engineers at FAANG companies earn ₹30–60 LPA all-in, but hiring is highly competitive.
  • Consulting firms (Accenture, Deloitte, KPMG) — mid-range, ₹12–22 LPA, with diverse project exposure and good career paths.

Remote Work and International Opportunities

One of the most significant trends of 2026 is the continued growth of remote DevOps roles with international salaries paid in USD or GBP. Indian DevOps engineers working remotely for US or UK companies typically earn $60,000 – $120,000 per year — equivalent to ₹50 – ₹100+ LPA — while living in India.

To be competitive for international remote roles, you typically need: AWS certification (Solutions Architect or DevOps Professional), Kubernetes experience, strong CI/CD skills, and the ability to work in English. Our students who have pursued this path consistently outpace their peers in local IT companies by 3–5x in earnings within 3–4 years.

How to Move Up the Salary Scale Faster

Based on what we have seen across 500+ students and our industry connections, here are the most effective ways to accelerate your DevOps salary growth:

  • Get certified: AWS Solutions Architect Associate is the highest-ROI certification — it consistently unlocks higher-paying roles and promotions.
  • Build visible projects: A GitHub portfolio with real CI/CD pipelines, Terraform modules, and Kubernetes deployments is more persuasive than any certificate.
  • Switch companies at 2–3 year intervals: Internal raises in Indian IT companies average 8–12% per year. External job offers typically deliver 30–50% salary jumps.
  • Specialize: Kubernetes, SRE practices, and cloud security are the highest-premium specializations in 2026. Generalists are essential early in your career; specialists command premium pay at mid and senior levels.
  • Target product companies: Even a step down in brand recognition from a large services company to a mid-size product startup can double your compensation, especially once you factor in equity.
